# Clinical Audit: Antibiotic Prescribing for Uncomplicated UTIs in Women

**Topic:** Adherence to NICE Guidelines (NG109) for treating uncomplicated UTIs in non-pregnant women under 65.
**Auditor:** Dr. Sarah Smith
**Dates:** Cycle 1 (Jan 2025) -> Cycle 2 (July 2025)

## 1. Standards (Criteria)
1. **First Line:** Nitrofurantoin 100mg MR BD for 3 days (or Trimethoprim if low resistance risk).
2. **Duration:** Prescription duration should be exactly 3 days.
3. **Urine Culture:** Should *not* be routinely sent for uncomplicated cases unless risk factors present.

## 2. Cycle 1: Baseline Data (Jan 2025)
- **Sample Size:** 20 patients.
- **Micro-teaching:** Conducted Feb 2025 to review NG109.

**Results:**
- **First Line Choice:** 90% compliant (2 prescribed Cephalexin inappropriately).
- **Duration (3 Days):** 60% compliant. (40% were given 5-7 days).
- **Culture Sent:** 30% Sent (Too high - unnecessary cost/workload).

**Analysis:**
GPs were habitually prescribing for 5 or 7 days based on old guidance.

**Intervention:**
1. Presented audit findings at clinical meeting (Feb 15, 2025).
2. Updated "Ardens" template in EMIS to default to 3 days for this code.
3. Distributed cheat sheet for antibiotic choices.

## 3. Cycle 2: Re-Audit (July 2025)
- **Sample Size:** 20 patients (new cohort).

**Results:**
- **First Line Choice:** 100% compliant.
- **Duration (3 Days):** 95% compliant. (Huge improvement).
- **Culture Sent:** 10% Sent (Acceptable - these had clinical rationale).

## 4. Conclusion & Actions
The intervention to update the clinical system templates was highly effective (60% -> 95% compliance on duration).
**Recommendation:** Re-audit in 1 year to ensure standards are maintained.
