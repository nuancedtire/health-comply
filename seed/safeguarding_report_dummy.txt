# Annual Safeguarding Report: 2023-2024

## Executive Summary
This report summarizes the safeguarding activities and incidents at Southside Medical Practice for the period of April 2023 to March 2024. Our commitment to protecting vulnerable adults and children remains a top priority.

## 1. Staff Training
- 100% of clinical staff have completed Level 3 Safeguarding training for both Children and Adults.
- 95% of non-clinical staff have completed Level 1 or 2 training.
- We hold monthly "Safeguarding Spotlight" sessions during our lunchtime meetings.

## 2. Incident Reporting
- Total Safeguarding Referrals: 14
  - Children: 8
  - Adults: 6
- All referrals were completed using the local authority MASH (Multi-Agency Safeguarding Hub) portal within 24 hours of the incident being identified.
- One significant incident involving a delay in referral was reviewed as a "Learning Event" in June 2023.

## 3. Multi-Agency Working
- We participate in the quarterly Local Safeguarding Board meetings.
- Dr. Roberts is our designated Safeguarding Lead and attends all Case Conferences (CPCs) and Looked After Children (LAC) reviews.

## 4. Compliance with CQC Standards
- Our processes align with the "Safe" key question, specifically the "Safeguarding" quality statement.
- We maintain a "Vulnerable Patient Register" which is reviewed and updated weekly by the nursing team.
